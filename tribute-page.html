Clarence Clemons

Rock n' Roll's Greatest Saxophonist

A Timeline of The Big Man's life:

  • 1942 - Born in Norfolk County, Virginia
  • 1951 - At the age of 9, Clarence receives an alto saxophone from his father for Christmas
  • 1960 - Attends Maryland State College on both a music and football scholarship
  • 1962 - While playing with the band, the Vibratones, Clarence moves to Somerset, New Jersey alongside a job counseling emotionally disturbed children
  • 1971 - Meets Bruce Springsteen and The Wonder Bar in Asbury Park, New Jersey
  • 1972 - Plays tenor saxophone on Springsteen's debut album Greetings from Asbury Park
  • 1975 - Records one of the greatest saxophone solos of all-time on "Jungleland"
  • 1977 - Appears in Martin Scorsese's movie, New York, New York as Cecil Powell
  • 1985 - Releases the solo album Hero with the hit single "You're a Friend of Mine"
  • 1989 - Acts as one of the "Three Most Important People In The World" in the film Bill & Ted's Excellent Adventure
  • 2011 - Experiences a final claim to fame playing on Lady Gaga's "Edge of Glory"
  • 2011 - Dies in West Palm Beach, Florida at the age of 69
  • 2014 - Posthumously inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame with the E Street Band

"Nobody captured my audience's imagination or their hearts like Clarence. Clarence was a figure out of a rock 'n' roll storybook, and together, we told a story that was bigger than any of the ones I had written in my songs"

-- Bruce Springsteen
